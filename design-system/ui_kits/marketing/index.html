The pillars

Three things this game is.

Messinia Guide is a contemplative tycoon — closer to a literary travel essay than to an arcade. Every screen serves three ideas.

01

The Peloponnese is the protagonist.

Voidokilia, Taygetos, Polylimnio, Neda — real places. Bring the geography forward; don't abstract it.

02

Instrument vs. prose.

Monospaced stats. Literary serif narrative. The contrast between the two voices is the whole UI rhythm.

03

Contemplative, not arcade.

No neon, no juicing. A tavern at dusk, leafing through a logbook of a season.

Six routes (and counting)

The arc

From one guide to an outfit.

The emotional core is the shift from doing the work to leading the people who do it. Three phases, one season at a time.

Phase I · live

The solo guide.

You pack. You meet the group. You read the weather and walk the trail. Every euro earned is a euro you carried home.

Phase II · live

The team.

Hire Maria, Nikos, Thalia. Assign trips, manage morale and fatigue. Crises come over the radio — you decide who answers.

Phase III · soon

Water and air.

Kayaks in Navarino Bay. Paragliders off Taygetos. Sailing, snorkelling. New gear, new certs, new weather to read.
