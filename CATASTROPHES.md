# CATASTROPHES.md — the heartbeat seedbank

Content for the **catastrophe heartbeat** through-line (`PHASE-ARCHITECTURE.md §4.3`). Not
final copy — a seedbank of disasters to draw, tune, and eventually turn into `EVENTS` /
`RADIO`-style data arrays (see `CLAUDE.md` for the array pattern).

**Tone rule (from §6):** mostly funny, occasionally a cold drop. Every entry is one a real
Messinia operator would recognise. The comedy is what earns the rare straight-faced ones.

**Rhythm:** *tremors* often, *seasonal shocks* sometimes, *earthquakes* rarely. Many entries
**scale** — the same disaster is a tremor or an earthquake depending on severity (the transfer
is the clearest example).

> Entries marked **[F]** came straight from Fotis.

---

## Tier 1 — Tremors (the ordinary weather of a season)

- **The canyon turns the colour of tea** — a storm broke upstream an hour ago, somewhere you
  couldn't see. The water is rising. Decide now.
- **Meltemi** — the gulf has gone white and angry. Every kayak and SUP booking is now,
  technically, a swimming lesson.
- **Kostas is "fine"** — your guide arrives the colour of feta, swears the wedding ended
  early, and breathes last night into your face.
- **The empty van** **[F]** — Maria's halfway to the put-in when the radio crackles: the
  wetsuits, the PFDs, the rope — all still folded neatly on the floor of the base. The clients
  are already there, in their swimsuits, smiling.
- **Ruled by a four-year-old** **[F]** — the parents booked a "moderate" route. The actual
  itinerary is now being set, minute to minute, by a toddler with strong and changing feelings
  about a particular rock.
- **The transfer, lightly** **[F]** — the minibus met a goat-truck on a one-lane bend. Nobody
  hurt, a wing mirror martyred, an hour gone, and a driver and a farmer now conducting a loud
  philosophical debate in the middle of the road.
- **Flip-flops on Taygetos** — he booked the summit route. He has packed sunglasses and a
  single warm beer. That is the inventory.
- **"We did a 5k once"** — the couple who described themselves as "very active" are sitting
  down. It has been four hundred metres.
- **The snake that was a stick** — it was a stick. The screaming was real. The group will not
  stop talking about the snake that was a stick.
- **The van says no** — flat tyre on the dirt road to the far trailhead, and the spare is the
  colour of a decision you regret.
- **The phone goes in the river** — someone leaned out for the perfect waterfall shot. The
  phone is now part of the waterfall. This is, somehow, your fault.
- **The taverna ambush** — lunch was meant to be forty minutes. The owner is the client's "new
  best friend." It is now ninety, and the light is going.
- **The dodgy souvlaki** — it has hit, mid-gorge, with no exit for an hour. Morale and dignity
  are both in freefall.
- **The runaway** — the group has quietly become a group of one fewer. Someone wandered off
  for "a better photo" and the radio is silent.
- **A one-star, perfectly timed** — *"Guide was knowledgeable BUT —"* and it lands the morning
  your biggest group of the week is deciding whether to book.
- **The stamp** — the permit needs a stamp from an office that is open Tuesdays, nine to
  eleven. It is Tuesday. It is eleven-oh-five.
- **The terminal dies** — the card reader has chosen the busiest morning of the year to show a
  single, eternal spinning wheel. It is cash, or it is faith.
- **Wasps have moved into the gear store** — specifically into the helmets. The group is
  waiting. The helmets are mandatory. The wasps are unmoved.
- **The boar** — there is a wild boar on the trail, and it has not read the part of the
  brochure about you being in charge.

## Tier 2 — Seasonal shocks (these can bend a month)

- **Red day** — the authorities declare extreme fire risk and close the forests. Your three
  best routes are illegal until further notice.
- **The turtle police** — Voidokilia is protected, the nesting wardens are out, and your
  favourite launch spot is suddenly a €2,000 question.
- **Heatwave** — forty-three degrees for a week. The trips that aren't dangerous are
  unbookable. The cicadas sound like a fax machine dying.
- **The drought** — the rivers are low and brown. Rafting is a gentle bump down some rocks;
  canyoning is a walk. The brochure promised more.
- **The mirror outfit** — a new competitor has appeared: your exact signature tour, fifteen
  euros cheaper, with a logo that's *almost* yours.
- **The van, robbed** — overnight, someone relieved you of four wetsuits, two paddles, and
  your good first-aid kit. The season is not waiting for you to re-stock.
- **The influencer** — 200k followers, behaves like a tax on patience, and is filming
  everything. This goes one of two ways and you don't pick which.
- **August, all at once** — every day fully booked, no slack, no spare guide, and a heat that
  makes mistakes. The month you make your year — or break a guide.
- **Nowhere to sleep** — the flat you lined up for your seasonal guides fell through, and in a
  tourist town in July there is not a spare bed for love nor money.
- **The algorithm blinks** — GetYourGuide reshuffles its rankings, or suspends your listing
  over a "policy," and your bookings simply stop arriving for a week.
- **The festival road** — a saint's-day panigiri (or a strike) has closed the only road to the
  trailhead. The whole village is dancing. You are not.
- **The audit letter** — ΑΑΔΕ would like to see every receipt since the company was a glint in
  your eye. You have twenty days and a shoebox.
- **myDATA, down** — the government invoicing portal is offline the day your filing is due. The
  fines accrue anyway. Greece is billing you for its own server.
- **The VAT reshuffle** — someone in Athens has reclassified what your tours technically *are*,
  and your margin just moved three points the wrong way, retroactively.
- **The processor freeze** — Viva has put your account "under review." The money is there. You
  cannot touch it. Payroll is Friday.
- **The 60-day float** — the OTA pays out two months after the trip, so all peak season you are
  quietly lending the platform a small fortune at zero interest.
- **The season slips** — spring came three weeks early and autumn won't leave; the calendar
  you've run for twenty years has quietly stopped fitting the mountain. *(A designated place to
  let the ache surface — §6.2: an old guide muttering "it wasn't like this.")*
- **The river, lower again** — the canyoning flow you built the summer on is thinner than last
  year, which was thinner than the year before.
- **The reality crew calls** — a production wants to film a "find yourself in the wild" series
  on your tour. The exposure would be enormous. So, you suspect, would the chaos.

## Tier 3 — Earthquakes (rare, and they define the year)

- **Fire** — a column of smoke where a region used to be. Roads shut, the air the colour of a
  bruise, and for a week nobody is hiking anywhere. *(Played straight. No joke here.)*
- **The accident** — someone is badly hurt, and after the fear comes the rest of it: the
  insurer, the reviews, the lawyer's letter, the quiet question of whether you read the weather
  wrong.
- **The transfer, badly** **[F]** — the airport transfer didn't reach the airport. The crash
  is serious; everyone lives, but the season tilts on its axis from this morning on.
- **Eleni hands in her radio** — your best guide, the nervous trainee you made into a
  professional, has been offered her own franchise by a rival. She'd like you to talk her out
  of it. You have until she finishes her coffee.
- **The strike** — ferries, fuel, or both. A group is stranded, a transfer is a fiction, and
  Greece, lovingly, shrugs.
- **The cruise blinks** — the line quietly drops Katakolo from its itinerary. A third of your
  July walked in off those ships. Walked.
- **The year that didn't happen** — a pandemic, a crash. The calendar empties. The mountains
  are gloriously, uselessly quiet.
- **The premium letter** — after the claim, your insurer renews you at a number with an extra
  digit. Or doesn't renew you at all.
- **The friend you have to fire** — a guide you love did something you can't unsee: drunk on
  the water, a client put at real risk. Friendship is not the question. Safety is.
- **The landlord sells** — the base — the office, the gear store, the yard you built — has been
  sold out from under you. You have ninety days.
- **The licence lapses** — your tourism-operator registration expired, the renewal is "being
  processed," and until a stranger in an office clicks a button you are, technically, not
  allowed to exist.
- **The forestry ruling** — the Δασαρχείο has decided your signature trail crosses protected
  forest. The route that made your name is now a court date.
- **The frozen account, in August** — the processor's hold lands at the peak: tens of thousands
  stranded, refunds you can't issue, and a queue of clients who only see that you've "taken
  their money and gone quiet."
- **The hundred-year flood, again** — the second one this decade tears out the trail, the
  put-in, and the little stone bridge everyone photographed. The climate has stopped asking
  permission.

## Tier 4 — The faintly absurd (disasters that are also anecdotes)

- **The goat with opinions** — it has decided the path is its path. It is not large. It is,
  however, extremely committed, and the group has already named it.
- **The proposal** — he's down on one knee at the summit, and from her face you can see this is
  a catastrophe. You are now a wedding planner and a hostage.
- **The "basically a guide"** — has watched a lot of YouTube, brought a map of a different
  mountain, and would like to lead.
- **The hen party** — eleven of them, matching shirts, one already crying, and the prosecco was
  a checked bag.
- **The naturist surprise** — the remote beach you promised was "completely private" is, today,
  enthusiastically populated.
- **You fall for a client** — it's the third day, they laughed at the goat thing, and a small
  dangerous part of you is doing arithmetic about the rest of your life. *(Feeds the
  runaway-romance ending — §8.3.)*
- **The cursed route** — a client "feels a terrible energy" and will not continue, and is now
  convincing the others that the gorge is haunted.
- **The antiquity** — the metal-detector enthusiast has found "something Mycenaean," and the
  day is now a question for the Ministry of Culture.
- **The sabotaged markers** — someone — surely not the mirror outfit — has been quietly
  rearranging the waymarks on your signature trail.
- **The proposal, to you** — a client, mid-hike, has decided you are The One. The remaining six
  kilometres are going to be very long.
- **The format** — a celebrity-survival show wants you as "the local expert," and only in the
  edit will you learn whether you were the hero or the punchline.
- **The staged rescue** — the crew wanted to film a dramatic rescue for the show. The dramatic
  rescue, unscripted, is now actually happening.
- **The drone meets the navy** — a client's drone has wandered toward a coastal military zone,
  and two very serious men in a jeep would like a word.
- **The battlefield** — the "couple's hike" is, it emerges around kilometre two, a divorce
  being conducted at altitude. You are the referee.

## To develop (when these become data)

- **Eligibility** — `when(b, w, S)` per entry: discipline, weather, phase, season-day,
  client type (a flash flood needs a water/gorge route; the cruise shock needs a tourist
  channel that exists).
- **Scaling** — entries that span tiers (the transfer, the storm, an injury) should pick
  severity from context rather than living in one tier.
- **Phase expression** — a tremor in Phase 1 is *you* on the trail; the same event in Phase 2
  is a `RADIO` call from a guide; in Phase 4+ it's a manager's phone call. Layer, don't
  replace (§3.1).
- **Choices & deltas** — each entry needs the 2–3 decisions and their outcome deltas (`rep`,
  `cash`, `morale`, `payMul`, `line`), in the existing `EVENTS` / `RADIO` shape.
- **Frequency weights** — tremors common, earthquakes rare; no two earthquakes in a season
  unless the design wants a "cursed year."
